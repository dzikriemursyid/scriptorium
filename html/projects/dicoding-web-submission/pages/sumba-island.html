Sumba Island: Explore the Enigmatic Charms of East Nusa Tenggara

Sumba Island, East Nusa Tenggara.

Welcome to Sumba Island, a land of rugged beauty and timeless tradition nestled in the heart of East Nusa Tenggara. Renowned for its pristine beaches, ancient megalithic sites, and vibrant cultural festivals, Sumba offers a unique glimpse into Indonesia's rich tapestry of heritage and natural wonders.

Megalithic Marvels

Step back in time as you journey through Sumba's megalithic landscape, where towering stone monuments known as "adat" stand as silent sentinels of the island's ancient past. Learn about the significance of these sacred sites, which serve as communal gathering spaces and repositories of ancestral wisdom.

Waikabubak and Waingapu

Discover the charming towns of Waikabubak and Waingapu, where traditional villages and bustling markets showcase the island's cultural diversity. Explore the iconic megalithic tombs of Prai Ijing and Pasunga, marveling at the intricate stone carvings and ancient rituals that have endured for centuries.

Sumba's Gastronomic Treasures

Indulge your senses with the flavors of Sumba's culinary heritage, where traditional dishes reflect a fusion of indigenous ingredients and cultural influences. Sample specialties such as "ikan bakar" (grilled fish), "sagu" (sago palm), and "ikan pepes" (steamed fish wrapped in banana leaves), infused with aromatic spices and served with warm hospitality.

Journey to the Heart of Sumba

Access Sumba Island via Tambolaka Airport or Waingapu Airport, both of which offer domestic flights from major cities in Indonesia. From the airport, travel by road to your desired destination, taking in the scenic beauty of Sumba's rugged coastline and lush hinterland along the way.
