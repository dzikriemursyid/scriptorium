Tanjung Puting: Journey into the Heart of Borneo's Pristine Rainforest

West Kotawaringin, Central Kalimantan.

Welcome to Tanjung Puting National Park, a pristine wilderness sanctuary nestled amidst the dense rainforests of Central Kalimantan, Borneo. Home to one of the largest populations of wild orangutans in the world, Tanjung Puting offers travelers a unique opportunity to observe these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat, along with a diverse array of wildlife, exotic flora, and breathtaking landscapes. Whether you're cruising along jungle rivers, trekking through ancient forests, or encountering playful primates at rehabilitation centers, Tanjung Puting promises an unforgettable journey into the heart of Borneo's untamed wilderness.

Orangutan Sanctuary

Embark on an unforgettable wildlife adventure to the orangutan sanctuaries of Tanjung Puting, where you can observe these fascinating primates up close in their natural rainforest habitat. Join guided tours to Camp Leakey, Tanjung Harapan, and Pondok Tanggui, where orangutans are rehabilitated and reintroduced into the wild, and witness their playful antics, social interactions, and remarkable intelligence. Learn about orangutan conservation efforts and the challenges facing these endangered species, and contribute to their protection and preservation through eco-friendly tourism practices.

River Cruises

Embark on a journey along the winding rivers and waterways of Tanjung Puting, where traditional klotok boats offer a unique vantage point for exploring the park's pristine rainforest landscapes. Cruise through dense mangrove swamps, tranquil blackwater tributaries, and towering peat swamp forests, encountering a wealth of wildlife along the way, from proboscis monkeys and gibbons to hornbills and pythons. Marvel at the natural beauty of Borneo's diverse ecosystems, with opportunities for birdwatching, wildlife photography, and jungle trekking at remote jungle campsites.

Research and Education

Support ongoing conservation efforts and environmental education programs in Tanjung Puting, aimed at protecting the park's fragile ecosystems and preserving its rich biodiversity for future generations. Learn about the work of local NGOs, research institutions, and park rangers in monitoring wildlife populations, restoring degraded habitats, and promoting sustainable tourism practices that minimize the impact on the environment. Participate in eco-friendly activities such as tree planting, wildlife monitoring, and educational workshops, and gain a deeper understanding of the interconnectedness of humans, wildlife, and the natural world.

Gateway to Borneo

Accessing Tanjung Puting National Park is relatively straightforward, with Pangkalan Bun's Iskandar Airport serving as the main gateway to Central Kalimantan. From Pangkalan Bun, travelers can take a short drive to Kumai, the starting point for boat trips into Tanjung Puting, where a fleet of traditional klotok boats awaits to transport visitors deep into the heart of the rainforest. Alternatively, domestic flights are available from Jakarta and other major cities to Pangkalan Bun, providing convenient access to Borneo's pristine wilderness.
