Derawan Island: Explore the Pristine Beauty of East Kalimantan's Tropical Gem

Berau, East Kalimantan.

Welcome to Derawan Island, a tropical paradise nestled in the azure waters of East Kalimantan. With its powdery white sand beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and vibrant coral reefs, Derawan Island beckons travelers to immerse themselves in its natural splendor. Whether you're seeking thrilling underwater adventures or simply a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, Derawan Island promises an unforgettable tropical getaway.

Traditional Villages and Coastal Communities

Immerse yourself in the laid-back island lifestyle as you visit the traditional villages and coastal communities of Derawan Island. Engage with local villagers and learn about their rich cultural heritage, from traditional fishing techniques to age-old customs and rituals. Experience the warmth of Indonesian hospitality as you share meals with villagers and gain insight into their way of life, deeply rooted in the rhythms of the sea and the land.

Snorkeling and Diving Haven

Dive into the pristine waters surrounding Derawan Island and discover a kaleidoscope of marine life teeming beneath the surface. Explore vibrant coral gardens, home to an array of colorful fish, sea turtles, and exotic creatures. Swim alongside majestic manta rays at Sangalaki Island, or venture to Kakaban Island to snorkel in the world-renowned Jellyfish Lake, where millions of harmless jellyfish pulsate in the water, creating a surreal underwater spectacle.

Seafood Gastronomy

Indulge your taste buds with the freshest seafood delicacies that Derawan Island has to offer. Feast on grilled fish, succulent prawns, and exotic shellfish, sourced straight from the bountiful waters surrounding the island. Savor the flavors of Indonesian cuisine as you dine al fresco by the beach, accompanied by breathtaking sunsets and the gentle lapping of waves against the shore.

Island Paradise Access

Accessing Derawan Island is relatively straightforward, with flights available from major cities in Indonesia to the nearby city of Tarakan in East Kalimantan. From Tarakan, travelers can arrange boat transfers to Derawan Island, with options ranging from public ferries to private speedboats. Sit back and enjoy the scenic journey as you cruise through the tranquil waters of the Sulawesi Sea, arriving at Derawan Island ready to embark on your tropical adventure.
